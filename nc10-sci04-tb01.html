Carbon and it's Compounds

General Properties of Carbon:

  • Symbol: C
  • Atomic Number: 6
  • Electronic Configuration: 1s2, 2s2, 2p2
  • Valency: 4
  • Carbon attains the noble gas configuration by sharing its valence electrons with other atoms like hydrogen, oxygen, nitrogen, chlorine, etc.
  • All living structures are carbon based.

Covalent Bonds:

These are the chemical bonds formed between atoms of a molecule by sharing valence electrons.

Properties of Covalent Compounds:

  1. Covalently bonded molecules have low melting and boiling points because of comparatively weaker intermolecular forces, unlike ionic compounds.
  2. These molecules are generally poor conductor of electricity as there are no free electrons to conduct electricity.
