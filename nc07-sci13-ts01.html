What are the two properties of carbon which lead to the huge number of carbon compounds we see around us?
The two properties of carbon which lead to the huge number of carbon compounds are:
(i) Catenation: It is the ability to form long chains by forming covalent bonds with other atoms of carbon.
(ii) Tetravalency: It is the ability of carbon to form 4 covalent bonds with atoms of other elements like O, Cl, H etc.

Why do ionic compounds have high melting points?
Ionic compounds are formed by the combination of ions.
Therefore, there is a lot stronger electrostatic force of attraction between negative and positive ions and hence they require a lot of energy to break ionic bonds. That is why ionic compounds have high melting points.
Why is the conversion of ethanol to ethanoic acid an oxidation reaction?
The only difference between molecule of ethanol (-OH) and Ethanoic Acid (-COOH) is the oxygen atom in ethanoic acid.
During this reaction an atom of oxygen get added to ethanol to convert it into ethanoic acid.
A mixture of oxygen and ethyne is burnt for welding. Can you tell why a mixture of ethyne and air is not used?
Ethyne is an unsaturated compound of carbon. During combustion it will not burn completely if sufficient oxygen is not supplied. The flame will thus become sooty which might reduce the quality of weld. Thus to get a blue, non-sooty flame, ethyne is burnt in presence of oxygen. $$\ce{C2H2 + O2 -> CO2 + H2O + \text{Heat}}$$
